Background

This project was a part of the Google UX certification program.

‘CoverWorld’ is a website for purchasing mobile covers online. It provides high-quality products to its customers with free delivery across the country. Not only does CoverWorld have a wide variety of design options, but it also lets its users customize the design according to their tastes.

People who buy customized phone covers will be the target audience of this project.

My Role

UI/UX designer designing the responsive website for CoverWorld from conception to delivery.

Responsibilities

  • Branding
  • Conducting usability studies
  • Conducting interviews
  • Accounting for accessibility
  • Paper and digital wireframing
  • Iterating on designs
  • Low and high-fidelity prototyping

Problem

Having competitors who also provided the option of customization.

Goal

To design a smooth and responsive UX (for mobile cover customization) than our competitors.

Framework Used

The framework used for this project was design thinking and all the steps (empathize, define, ideate, prototype, test) were followed.

User Research Summary

The research was conducted and data was collected for a better understanding of the user needs. It suggested that for most users the biggest problem while ordering phone covers was having little to no freedom of customizing the design.

Also, users were quite annoyed by the fact that the covers were always displayed on an iPhone device which restricted them to imagine how the product will look on other devices.

Lastly, there was a group of users who shopped from the Instagram pages so the whole ordering process would take longer due to the delay in communication with the seller.

User (Research) Pain Points

1
No customization option
Often there was no design customization option and the user had to choose from given design options.
2
Only Iphone device mockups
Most of the time, the product was displayed on an iPhone device, making it hard for users to see how the cover will look on other devices.

Digital Wireframes

As I moved to digital wireframing, I kept in mind to incorporate findings from the user research.

The main user flow was the customization process so I tried to make it a smooth experience.

Usability study (findings)

I conducted one round of usability study and the findings from that study helped guide the designs.

  • Users found it hard to find the available design options.
  • They found the customization process smooth.

Refining the Design

During the usability study, it was discovered that the users had difficulty finding and browsing the available design options. The problem was then resolved by making two new sections (one for browsing designs and the other for customization) on the home page.

    Color contrast ratio

  • 3
    Imagery

Impact

The website makes users feel like CoverWorld considers how to make the overall experience better for them.

What I learned?

While designing the CoverWorld website, I learned that user research and feedback should not be underestimated. Usability studies and peer feedback influence each iteration of the designs.
